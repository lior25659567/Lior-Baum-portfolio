Line 2 — The one differentiator that makes a SaaS hiring manager read on: complex workflow simplification with a clinical-credibility proof point, now targeting B2B SaaS product companies.

**Draft direction (copy-writer fills in the exact language):**

## 2. Experience — Per-Role Guidance

### Align Technology — UX/UI Designer (Dec 2024 – Present)
**Current bullets: 3. Keep: 3. Rewrite all 3 — none land.**

**Bullet 1 (current):** "Improved key parts of the iTero™ scanner experience, focusing on how doctors and patients move through the system."
- "Improved key parts" is the softest possible phrasing. Which parts? Improved how? By what measure?
- "focusing on how doctors and patients move through the system" — describes scope, not achievement.
- **Rewrite direction:** Name the specific flow worked on, name what was wrong, name the outcome. "Redesigned the [scan setup / patient onboarding / post-scan review] flow for [role], reducing [step count / error rate / time] by [ADD METRIC]." If no metric exists, at minimum make the scope concrete: "Redesigned the end-to-end scan workflow — from patient onboarding through post-scan clinical review — for [N clinics / N users / the global iTero platform]."
- **[ADD METRIC]** — time-on-task, drop-off rate, step reduction, NPS, or scope (DAU, clinics, countries).

**Bullet 2 (current):** "Redesigned navigation and content structure within the iTero™ Store to enhance discoverability and usability of scanner options and accessories."
- Better than bullet 1 — names the surface and the goal. Still no outcome.
- "enhance discoverability and usability" is vague. Discoverability went up by what signal?
- **Rewrite direction:** Keep the surface (iTero Store navigation), add the outcome signal. "Redesigned navigation and content taxonomy in the iTero™ Store, [reducing search time for accessories by X% / increasing add-to-cart rate by X% / cutting support tickets about product discoverability]."
- **[ADD METRIC]** — any downstream conversion, support ticket reduction, or user testing result.

**Bullet 3 (current):** "Designed the full UX experience around scanning – from patient onboarding and scan setup to post-scan insights and next steps."
- This is an overlap with bullet 1 — both are about the scanning experience. Either merge them or make them clearly distinct surfaces.
- "full UX experience" — scope claim with no scale. Full for how many users? How many steps reduced?
- **Rewrite direction:** If this is truly a separate workstream from bullet 1, make the distinction explicit. If it overlaps, merge bullets 1 and 3 into a single stronger bullet that names the full scope + one concrete outcome, freeing a bullet slot for something distinct.
- After merge or distinction, this slot should carry the strongest outcome number on the resume. **[ADD METRIC]**

**Title note:** The job title at Align is "UX/UI Designer." The resume title is "Product & UX Designer." If the actual job title was UX/UI Designer, list it that way — ATS systems match exact title strings. If the internal title or contract was something else, list what is defensible.

## 6. Red Flags a Recruiter Would Notice

- **Title mismatch:** Resume title is "Product & UX Designer" but every job listing says "UX/UI Designer." A senior-track candidate targeting "Senior Product Designer" roles should carry that title consistently, or the mismatch reads as self-promotion disconnected from reality. Consider: "Senior Product Designer" or "Product Designer" if the work supports it, and align all job titles to what was on the contract.

- **No numbers anywhere.** A resume with zero metrics for a designer claiming B2B SaaS experience at a company the size of Align Technology is a yellow flag. Even approximate figures ("redesigned a flow used by 3,000+ dental practices globally") are better than nothing. The absence of any quantification signals either that outcomes were not tracked, or that the designer is not thinking in business terms.

- **Level inconsistency:** "Intern" at WizeCare (2023) → "UX/UI Designer" at BigIdea (4 months, 2024) → "UX/UI Designer" at Align (2024–present) is a plausible progression, but the resume title of "Product & UX Designer" and the target of "Senior Product Designer" represents a full-level jump that the bullet quality does not yet support. The fix is not to drop the ambition — it is to write bullets that close the gap.

- **Teaching Assistant role at senior-track level:** Listed as a full concurrent role alongside Align Technology. For a senior-track product design resume, this dilutes the signal. A recruiter scanning quickly sees two concurrent roles and briefly wonders what the commitment to the Align role looks like. Condense or remove.

- **"Currently exploring"** in the summary: "exploring" is a passive, junior-signaling word on a resume targeting senior roles. Cut it.
