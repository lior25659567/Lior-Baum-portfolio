# Synthesis — The Techno Chronicles (project-1776617213367)

Unified verdict from the ux-reviewer, design-recruiter, and design-director passes,
grounded in `_designer-profile.md` (target: Senior Product Designer, B2B SaaS,
workflow-heavy products).

## Where all 3 agents agree

These are the highest-confidence, non-negotiable fixes — every reviewer raised them independently:

1. **Add `metaItems` + `headlineMetric` to the intro slide.** Role, Timeline, Context (self-initiated), Tools (Figma + Webflow). This is the #1 recruiter scan signal and it is entirely missing. Cheapest, highest-impact change in the deck.
2. **Add a `reflection` slide before the `end`.** Required above mid-level. A personal project is the ideal place for an honest one: one transferable skill, one thing you'd do differently, one thing you couldn't measure.
3. **The `outcomes` slide is misused.** Rhythm / Balance / Responsive / Clarity are design properties, not outcomes. Retype to `reflection` (or recast as explicit design principles) — do not present them as results.
4. **Three consecutive `textAndImage` (problem) slides (1, 2, 3) are redundant.** Same structure, same "rhythm" framing repeated. Collapse/repurpose — at least one should become a `directions` (Ideation) slide showing paths considered + rejected.
5. **The `media` slide (Slide 4) is empty** — only a "Final Design" label. Add a title and a one-line description/highlight so the strongest visual asset isn't unframed.
6. **No decision rationale anywhere.** The interesting choices (ASCII portraits, subgenre radar, monochrome grain, scroll-telling) live only in the images and are never explained. Surface at least one "I chose X over Y because Z."
7. **Reposition the project.** All three flag that a personal editorial site sits awkwardly next to the clinical SaaS work. It must be explicitly framed as a craft / scroll-storytelling side project demonstrating a named transferable skill — not presented as a peer to iTero/WizeCare.

## Conflicts — DECIDED

**Keep this study in the portfolio, or cut/move it?**
Recruiter: "Maybe" — keep as a range piece if reframed. Director: "Needs Rework" — rework or move to an explorations section. UX: would be skipped as-is.
→ **Verdict: Keep it, but reframe — do not cut.** Grounded in the profile's Senior-SaaS target: range and craft are assets *provided* the deck doesn't pretend to be product work. The fix is positioning + the missing senior signals (metaItems, reflection, directions), not deletion. It earns its place as the "I also have expressive visual/interaction range" piece.

**Slide 3 (responsive) — retype to `comparison`, or fold into a `directions` slide?**
UX wants `comparison` (desktop/tablet/mobile panels); Director wants Slides 2-3 merged into one `directions` slide.
→ **Verdict: Fold Slides 2–3 into a single `directions` (Ideation) slide.** Showing rejected paths is the single fastest senior-judgment signal and resolves the redundancy at the same time. Keep one responsive visual as supporting imagery inside it rather than spending a whole slide on breakpoints.

**How hard to lean on "this is a personal project"?**
→ **Verdict: Lean in, honestly.** Name the absence of a brief/client/research as the *premise* ("I invented my own constraints"), which reframes missing research from a weakness into a deliberate creative-brief framing — and makes the reflection slide land.
