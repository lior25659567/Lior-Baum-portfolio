# Director Verdict — ResponsiveView (project-1780418394707)
**Strong Draft.** Argues for a tool-builder; needs to argue for a Senior product designer.

## Architecture
- Hook 7/10 — best sentence in the deck; opens in the classroom, not the product world.
- Stakes 4/10 (weakest) — "students had bad demos" is low-stakes for a SaaS audience; the portable principle (tools that live where the work happens get adopted) is buried in the reflection.
- Conflict 5/10 — directions slide is real conflict; but whatFailed is empty → no tension in the build.
- Resolution 6/10 — outcomes all `[ADD:]`; no closure that the problem was solved.
- Voice 8/10 — clear, first-person, warm; "the goal wasn't a technically complete extension — it was to change a habit" is the POV (buried at the end).

## Positioning
Reads as a dev-tool side project. The transfer to SaaS IS available (friction removal, adoption, behavior change, designing for a user who avoids the tool) but must be made explicit. Keep the honest "personal project" meta, but make the thinking read Senior.

## Top 3 rewrites
1. Move the transferable insight from reflection → the insight slide (3) and frame it as a universal principle ("remove the switching cost and you remove the adoption problem").
2. Fill `whatFailed` with one real failure (a confusing early UI, a cut feature, a webview API constraint).
3. Add one sentence to outcomes reframing success as behavior change (panel open during live coding, not just downloads).

## The signature move (latent)
"Designing a tool whose measure of success is whether people actually use it, not whether it's technically complete." Point the cover + insight + outcomes at it.

> Flag for UX: "52 devices" on 3 slides; whatFailed empty (structural, not just placeholder); outcomes need behavioral specifics.
