# Director Verdict — Design System in Claude Code (project-1776628169716)

### Career narrative
A genuinely differentiated concept underneath — a designer who treats a design system as governance and worked with AI as a structured collaborator. But the deck doesn't tell that story; it gives a tour (layers → components → surfaces → the AI bit). After reading I can't answer "what was the designer's superpower here?" The profile's claimed superpower (making complex things simple via a consistent mental model) never appears as an applied moment.

### Case study architecture
- **Hook 3/10** — describes the solution, not a problem; nothing makes me fear what happens without this system.
- **Stakes 2/10** — no before-state, no consequence, no cost. Outcomes imply slowness/drift existed but we never see it.
- **Conflict 1/10 (critical failure)** — zero tension: no constraint named, no direction rejected, no tradeoff. Slide 5 even defuses conflict ("AI didn't replace the work"). The skipped "05" label hints the hard-part slide is missing.
- **Resolution 4/10** — outcomes attempt a senior framing ("the designer's role changed") but are adjective-only with no figures, and circle back to no stated problem.
- **Voice 5/10** — clean, occasionally sharp ("Switching is a flag — not a rebuild") but the decision-making human is absent (passive: "all governed by the same constraints" — by whom? with what tradeoffs?). Unexplained jargon violates the profile rule.
- **Weakest: Conflict.** Fix first — without tension everything else is cosmetic.

### Seniority positioning gap
Current: **Mid** (method + output, no visible judgment). Target: **Senior.** One change closes most of it: a slide before Architecture establishing the concrete before-state (what broke, who was affected, the cost), plus naming one decision + its rejected alternative in the Architecture slide. Second gap: a **reflection** slide (required) — the system currently appears fully-formed and self-evidently correct, the most junior possible read.

### Market positioning
Optimized (unintentionally) for a design-systems-specialist role at a design-literate org. The target SaaS audience may not know Storybook/tokens and is evaluating product-design judgment — and there is no end user anywhere in this deck (the clinician/practitioner the product serves never appears). The intro images show a real product (dashboards, patient lists) — that product and its user should anchor the story; the system should be the solution to a human problem, not the subject.

### The signature move
Buried but real: "a system that teaches itself — new designers inherit the rules" + "designers own intent, AI accelerates execution." Nobody else has exactly this story. It never surfaces because the stakes are never made clear. The signature would be: "I design systems that govern themselves, so the team after me doesn't need me to maintain consistency." Not said anywhere yet.

### 3 rewrites that would change everything
1. **Give the problem a body** — replace the trend observation with the concrete before-state at Align (diverging scanner/web libraries? Figma↔production drift? a platform-switch maintenance crisis?) + a specific consequence.
2. **Show the hardest decision in the Architecture slide** — one rejected alternative (third-party system? Figma-first? separate libraries?) + a one-sentence why. Use the `directions` template.
3. **Add a `reflection` slide before end** — move the end-slide's "my role has changed" line into it; whatWorked / whatFailed / whatYoudDoDifferently / whatYouCouldntMeasure (specific, not philosophical).

### Slide order & missing beats
Order: 0 intro · 1 Context · 2 Architecture · 3 System · 4 Surfaces · 5 AI · 6 Outcomes · 7 end (end correctly last). Missing canonical beats: Problem (concrete), Research, Key Insights, Design Exploration (`directions`), Iteration, Process Timeline, **Reflection**. The "05" gap is where one of these belonged.
Recommended ops: INSERT reflection before end; INSERT a concrete problem/before-state slide near the front; INSERT a `directions` slide between Architecture and System; add `metaItems` to intro. (All new narrative content the designer must supply — `[ADD: …]`, never fabricated.)

### Director Verdict: Needs Rework
Differentiated raw material telling the wrong story (a system described, not a problem solved). Add the before-state, one decision + rejected alternative, and a reflection — those three convert it from artifact tour to senior case study. But the substance must come from the designer (no context provided) — fill via the Context tab, then re-fix.
