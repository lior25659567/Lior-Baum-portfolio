# Recruiter Verdict — Design System in Claude Code (project-1776628169716)
**Target:** Senior Product Designer, B2B SaaS, workflow-heavy.

### 4-minute first impression
Cover hook is sharp ("tokens, components, and rules shared by designers, developers, and AI — one source of truth"). But within 30s: there's no problem, no before-state, no tension — every post-cover slide describes what the system contains, like a product README. Images confirm the work is real (Align-branded Storybook with WEB/SCANNER toggle + token pages; polished product UI). An hour later I'd remember "built a design system in Claude Code" — but not why, for whom, or what changed.

### Role & level signal
Claims infrastructure-level scope (tokens, governance, AI collaboration = senior/staff responsibility) but presents it deliverable-first ("here's what I built"), which reads mid-level. The "AI as collaborator" framing fits a design-tools/startup or conference-talk audience more than the B2B-SaaS-workflow target. The designer is invisible in their own work — no decision, rejected direction, or constraint shown.

### Red flags
- No problem statement; no goals/success criteria.
- Outcomes are adjectives (Faster/Fewer/Changed) — no baseline/timeframe/n.
- No research or consumer (developer/designer) insight; no adoption data.
- Section numbering skips "05" (a slide deleted/never written, labels not cleaned).
- 5 consecutive `problem`-type slides for non-problem content — monotone.
- **No reflection slide** (required above mid).
- Empty `metaItems` — no role/timeline/team/tools.
- Outcomes title "What this project reflects about design today" = think-piece, not results.
- Jargon unexplained (Storybook, tokens, semantic tokens) — fails the profile's non-designer rule.

### Green flags
Strong positioning line; real, detailed Storybook + product screenshots (not mockups); genuine multi-surface systems thinking (scanner touch-first vs web dense/keyboard, theme at the token layer); a confident, differentiated AI-collaboration POV ("AI didn't replace the work, it amplified it").

### Level calibration
Raw material could support **L5** (multi-surface token system + AI-assisted dev for clinical SaaS is senior scope), but presentation reads **L4** — outputs not decisions, no numbers, no reflection, no cross-functional influence (could be a solo side project for all the deck shows). Push to L5: a one-slide problem with before-state + business cost, plus one real number.

### Culture fit
Signals design-tools / platform-team / design-eng-hybrid fit more than a product pod where one designer owns a user workflow. For the target, the deck should show how the system *enabled* a product outcome, not how it was constructed.

### Would you advance? — Maybe
Strong enough if a concrete before-state + one real outcome number are added; without them it's a product showcase, not a case study.

### Recruiter Verdict: Maybe
Deciding factor: no problem with stakes — nothing at risk, so nothing proven.

> **Flag for UX Reviewer:** Storybook shots on the intro are sparse/decorative and not connected to any slide's narrative — strong proof being wasted. The video-free feature slides need expressive component shots.
> **Flag for Director:** The deck competes as both "I think about design infrastructure" and "I ship polished SaaS UI" and wins neither for the target. Reframe around the product team/users the system served, with architecture as supporting evidence.
