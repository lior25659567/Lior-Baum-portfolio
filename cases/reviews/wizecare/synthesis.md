# WizeCare ‚Äî Synthesis

Three independent reviews (UX craft, recruiter screen, design-director positioning).
Scores: **UX 6.5/10**, **Recruiter "Maybe"**, **Director "Strong Draft."** They agree
far more than they differ: the *thinking* is senior-grade, but the case is an unfinished
draft and that's what's holding it back.

## Where all 3 agents agree (highest-confidence, non-negotiable)

1. **Slide 0 is the worst wound.** Role, timeline, team, tools, and the headline metric
   are all `[FILL IN]`. A reviewer can't infer seniority ‚Äî or even whether you led or
   executed ‚Äî in the 6-second scan. All three call this the single most damaging gap.
2. **Ownership is ambiguous: "I" vs "we" drifts across the deck.** The voice goes
   first-person and *alive* exactly where the work is most senior (Slides 10, 13) and
   hides behind "we" elsewhere. All three want one committed first-person ownership voice.
3. **The four outcome metrics are impressive but unsupported.** 75% fewer misclicks, 80%
   adoption among former non-users, 55% fewer tickets ‚Äî sourced as "per stakeholder
   report" with no n, no baseline, no timeframe. All three read large round numbers with
   no method as a *credibility risk*, not a strength.
4. **Pervasive `[FILL IN]` placeholders make it un-showable as-is.** ~a third of the
   load-bearing fields are blank ‚Äî including the research quotes that are the emotional core.
5. **Two missing beats cap the seniority read: `directions` (Ideation) and `reflection`.**
   UX and Director name both explicitly; the recruiter's level gap is the same gap. The
   solution space appears without alternatives, and the deck ends on "it worked" with no
   honest self-critique.
6. **Slides 6 and 14 are duplicate, fully-blank `process` slides** ‚Äî the validation
   methodology that would prove the outcomes is the load-bearing missing piece.
7. **The signature is real and under-sold: one reusable card system across all three
   flows = "new tool feels like one you already know."** Director frames it as the thesis;
   UX lists it as the standout; recruiter remembers the Protocol Builder for it. It's
   currently buried in `highlight`/`afterDescription` footnotes ‚Äî surface it as the spine.

## Top 5 action items, ordered by impact

1. **Fix Slide 0 and own it in first person.** Fill Role (a senior title), Timeline, Team,
   Tools, and a `headlineMetric`; rewrite the description as one owned, quantified sentence
   with zero brackets. This moves the seniority read more than anything else in the deck.
2. **Ground the outcomes + reconcile the research counts.** Give each of the four metrics an
   n, a baseline, and a timeframe; fill the duplicate validation/process slide with the real
   method; and make the interview count identical everywhere (Slide 2 says 12, Slide 3 says 8).
3. **Standardize a first-person, decision-owning voice** across all 17 slides ‚Äî extend the
   Slides 10/13 voice, don't invent it.
4. **Add `directions` (Ideation) and `reflection` slides.** Ideation before the Protocol
   Builder (2‚Äì3 explored directions, accepted/rejected + why); reflection before `end`
   (what worked, what you'd change, and what you couldn't measure). These close the mid‚Üísenior gap.
5. **Structural cleanup + surface the thesis.** Remove one duplicate `process` slide, merge
   Slides 3+4 and convert off the undocumented `researchFindings` (‚Üí `quotes`), and pull the
   card-system-reuse idea out of footnotes into a named thesis the whole redesign hangs on.

## Seniority signal

**Mid now ‚Äî with unmistakable Senior instincts; Senior is reachable.** (UX: "Mid with
Senior-level instincts"; Recruiter: "strong L4 / borderline L5"; Director: "Mid reading
toward Senior.") The constraint-aware, tradeoff-explicit decisions and the method-changing
research pivot are senior-grade; the unfinished cover, unsubstantiated metrics, and missing
exploration/reflection beats are what keep it from reading as finished senior work.

## Overall verdict

**Needs Work** ‚Äî but the hard part (the thinking) is already done. The single most important
fix: **fill the role on Slide 0 and ground the four outcome metrics.** Resolve the
placeholders, add the two missing beats, and commit to first-person ownership, and this
becomes a memorable senior clinical-SaaS case rather than a strong-but-unfinished draft.
